# 🤖 Dead Blockchain Theory

What if the Dead Internet Theory is happening on blockchains too?

Dead Blockchain Theory is a research project that explores a simple question:

**How much of blockchain activity is actually created by humans?**

Today, most blockchain ecosystems are evaluated using metrics such as transaction count, active wallets, and TVL. While these numbers are useful, they do not explain who is generating the activity.

A network can have millions of transactions and still be heavily driven by bots, automated systems, arbitrage tools, or farming activity.

This project was built to look beyond traditional metrics and explore the human side of blockchain adoption.

## The Idea

The idea came after discovering the concept of the Dead Internet Theory while scrolling through X.

Dead Internet Theory suggests that a large portion of internet activity may be generated by bots rather than real people.

That raised another question:

**What if the same thing is happening on-chain?**

Instead of measuring only activity, what if we tried to measure human participation?

## What Does It Do?

Dead Blockchain Theory analyzes different blockchain networks and estimates:

* Bot Activity
* Human Activity
* Suspicious Wallet Behavior
* Automation Signals
* Deadness Score

The platform provides a simple dashboard where users can compare networks and explore activity patterns.

## Important Note

This project does not attempt to prove that a wallet is a bot.

The metrics shown are estimates based on observable on-chain behavior patterns.

The goal is not to label a chain as good or bad.

The goal is to provide a different perspective on blockchain activity.

The project is designed to work with on-chain analytics providers such as:

* Dune Analytics
* Public blockchain data
* Explorer APIs
* Custom research queries

The dashboard uses transaction activity, wallet behavior, and interaction patterns to generate insights.

## Why It Matters

As blockchain ecosystems grow, transaction counts alone become less meaningful.

Bots, AI agents, MEV systems, and automated tools are becoming increasingly common.

Understanding the difference between activity and human participation may become one of the most important metrics in the future.

## Project Vision

We already measure:

* TVL
* Transactions
* Active Wallets

Maybe it's time to measure humans too.
